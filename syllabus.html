Course Description

Interactive media design and development for internal and external community clients. Design solutions are presented, critiqued, and revised based on initial and ongoing client contact. Sites are developed, deployed, tested, and maintained on web servers. Team-based design and development process is coordinated through project management practices. Emphasis is placed on strategic and tactical design process, industry standards, usability studies, business proposals, design documents, and other professional practices.

Organization
Discovery
Topics related to initial interactions with a potential client.
Definition
Topics related to the broad definition of an interactive product, including drafting a statement of definition.
Design
Create wireframes and mockups which demonstrate expected behavior. Provide clients with multiple options upfront.
Development
Develop a working site to the decided specifications on an incremental schedule.
Delivery
How to deliver a working site frequently and incrementally.
Course Topics
  • Roles: business, creative, technical (the minimum team)
  • Objectives: each roles has its objectives within the process
  • Deliverables: business documents, artwork, prototypes
  • Activities: project management, design, production, engineering, maintenance
  • Client: the simple client, the composite client, and the surrogate client
  • Initial preparations
  • Survey of current skills, interests, and achievements of individual students
  • Formation of teams based on survey results
  • Team selection or assignments to client projects
Ethics & Conduct

In a perfect world, this section of the syllabus wouldn't exist. There are truly only two words worth mentioning here: *respect* and *honesty*. All I ask is that you treat myself and your fellow students with the respect with which you would wish to be treated yourself. By the same token, I also ask that you display honesty when completing in-class assignments and work outside of class.

Attendance

Attendance is mandatory in order to foster diverse class discussion and allow for constructive in-class teamwork. Arriving late, leaving early, or failing to attend a class will adversely affect your grade. Students are allowed one excused absence per term to allow for illness or personal circumstance. Subsequent absences will result in a 5% reduction in your final grade.
